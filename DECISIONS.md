# Design Notes
---
## Questions for the Client
### Invoice Lifecycle
- Can invoices be modified after approval or sending?
- Are there restrictions on backdating invoices?
### Approval Workflow
- Are approvals required for all invoices, or only under certain conditions?
- Should approval be single-level or multi-level?
- Are there approval thresholds based on invoice amount?
### Payments and Billing
- Should invoices support partial payments?
- How should outstanding balances be calculated in case of multiple payments?
### Adjustments and Credits
- Should adjustments and credits be applied at the invoice level or line-item level?
- Are credit notes required as separate entities?
### Access Control
- Should staff have access to all clients and projects, or only those assigned to them?
### Sending Invoices
- Should invoices be sent via email or integrated with external systems?
### Reporting
- What level of reporting is required (invoice, project, client)?
- Are there requirements for time-based reporting (e.g. monthly summaries, overdue invoices)?

## Assumptions
- Authentication is mocked via request headers.
- A single-level approval workflow is used, where submitted invoices are reviewed by users with the manager role.
- Invoices follow a simple lifecycle from draft to approval and delivery, with payment status simplified as unpaid or paid.
- Invoices can only be edited while in a draft state.
- Basic access control is applied based on user role and ownership.
- Payments are simplified, and partial payments are not supported.
- Adjustments and credits are applied at the invoice level.
- Invoice identifiers are unique and generated by the system.
- Dates (e.g. issue date and due date) are user-provided without strict validation constraints.
- Currency and tax handling are simplified and assumed to be consistent across invoices.
- Reporting is based on invoice data, with aggregation used for higher-level summaries.
- Invoice delivery is represented as a system action (no external delivery integration).
- Full audit logging is not implemented; only basic metadata is recorded.

## If I Had More Time
- Add a proper user system with JWT-based authentication and role management.
- Add more granular access control, such as limiting invoice visibility by user, role, client, or project assignment.
- Add a payments table to support detailed billing history, partial payments, and remaining balance tracking.
- Extend the approval workflow to support multi-level approval and simple business rules, such as allowing low-value invoices to bypass approval.
- Add a dashboard task list to surface workflow events, such as invoices awaiting approval or rejected invoices that require user action.
- Enhance reporting capabilities (e.g. filtering by client, project, or date range).
- Improve validation, error handling, and edge case coverage.
