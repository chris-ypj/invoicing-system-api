# Design Notes
The system is designed with a focus on simplicity, correctness, and clear workflow control.
Complex features are intentionally deferred to keep the core system maintainable and extensible.
---
## Questions for the Client
### Invoice Lifecycle
- Can invoices be modified after approval or sending?
- Are there restrictions on backdating invoices?
### Approval Workflow
- Are approvals required for all invoices, or only under certain conditions?
- Should approval be single-level or multi-level?
- Are there approval thresholds based on invoice amount?
### Payments and Billing
- Should invoices support partial payments?
- How should outstanding balances be calculated in case of multiple payments?
### Adjustments and Credits
- Should adjustments and credits be applied at the invoice level or line-item level?
- Should credit notes be separate records?
### Access Control
- Should staff have access to all clients and projects, or only those assigned to them?
### Sending Invoices
- Should invoices be sent via email or integrated with external systems?
### Reporting
- What level of reporting is required (invoice, project, client)?
- Are there requirements for time-based reporting (e.g. monthly summaries, overdue invoices)?

## Assumptions
- Authentication is mocked via request headers.
- A single-level approval workflow is used, where submitted invoices are reviewed by users with the manager role.
- Invoices follow a simple lifecycle from draft to approval and delivery, with payment status simplified as unpaid or paid.
- Invoices can only be edited while in a draft state.
- Basic access control is applied based on user role and ownership.
- Payments are simplified, and partial payments are not supported.
- Adjustments and credits are applied at the invoice level.
- Invoice identifiers are unique and generated by the system.
- Dates (e.g. issue date and due date) are user-provided without strict validation constraints.
- Currency and tax handling are simplified and assumed to be consistent across invoices.
- Reporting is available at both invoice and project levels.
- Sending an invoice only updates its status (no external delivery integration).

## If I Had More Time
- Add a proper user system with authentication and role-based access control.
- Support partial payments and improve billing accuracy.
- Extend the approval workflow with more flexible business rules.
- Improve reporting capabilities (e.g. filtering and summaries).
- Improve billing history tracking with more detailed payment records.
- Strengthen validation and error handling.

---
## Production Considerations
- Add proper login and permissions (e.g. JWT authentication and role management).
- Use a production database (e.g. PostgreSQL or DanamoDB) instead of SQLite.
- Ensure invoice amounts are calculated correctly and prevent invalid workflow actions.
- Support partial payments and track remaining balances accurately.
- Store invoice files using object storage (e.g. S3), with cold storage (e.g. Glacier) for infrequent access.
- Add approval history, audit logging, and centralised system logging for traceability, monitoring, and workflow transparency.
- Set up deployment and automate build/release (CI/CD), choosing infrastructure based on scale and budget.
- Manage sensitive configuration securely using environment variables and secrets management (e.g. AWS Secrets Manager).
